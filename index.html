Agile

What is Agile?

  • Able to move quickly and easily
  • Able to think and understand quickly

Agile is the collective name for management techniques that

  • Focus the team on the needs and goals of customers.
  • Simplify organizational structure and processes.
  • Offer work in short cycles.
  • Actively use feedback.
  • Are based on a humanistic approach.
  • Are not the final state, but rather a way of thinking and living.

Focus on customer needs and goals

Work tools

short-lived, but intense sessions (meetings) of all participants in the work or the key majority, where various ideas are generated and tested.

Short cycle operation

iteratively incremental approach

  • Work is carried out in small fixed periods of time, for example, in one, two or four weeks
  • At the end of each period of time is created not just some kind of intermediate result, but a small, stripped down, but workable version of the product that you can already start using.

All Agile process approaches have short cycles

  • Nexus
  • LeSS (Large-Scale Scrum)
  • SAFe
  • XP
